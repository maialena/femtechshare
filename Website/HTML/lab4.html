V. Video in HTML5

If you want to feature a video from YouTube or some video hosting site, you can easily do so by embedding it. Nowadays, many sites make this easy. You can often right-click on the video, choose "Copy embed code" and paste it into your html doc. What's copied is something like this: <iframe width="640" height="360" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/J---aiyznGQ" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe>

Simply adjust the width and height attributes to the appropriate size, and you're done. 'Frameborder' is a boolean attribute (meaning it has two values, "on" or "of") that indicate whether or not you want to have a border. 0 is "off", 1 is "yes."

Say you have a .mp4 or .ogv file on your computer (say something you've filmed yourself, or an animated clip you've made with Flash) that you want to include on your website. Again, the easiest way to do so is to host the video through a site like Youtube and embed it on your site as above. The upside is that video hosting sites will automatically convert your video into the various formats required by browsers. The downside of that is if you want your video to play exclusively on your website. If that's the case, you'll have to host it, which takes a few more steps

The main consideration when hosting your own video is including all the various formats supported by different browsers, including AVI, Flash, MPEG, Quicktime, and more. Fortunately, HTML5 (which unfortunately is not supported by all browsers) makes this easy with its <video> element. If you're interested in using HTML5 to embed video into your page, use the following links (ordered by usefulness) to learn more:

VI. Audio in HTML5

As with video, there are different ways to embed audio into your webpage. The first is through a hosted service like Soundcloud.com that allows you to upload your audio; this route also provides an audio player that you can embed in your page - making it really easy. The second method is harder, and relies on Flash. The third is through using HTML5's built-in <audio> element. To delve into any of these methods, use the following links:
