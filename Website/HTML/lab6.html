OVERVIEW

We're going to talk about perhaps the most important concept in web design and development: LAYOUT. A good layout is readable and highlights the most important information on the page. Here are 7 Pro Layout Tips. to get you thinking about how you want to structure your page.

We'll take a closer look at something called 'responsive web design.' Responsive web design is the about making your content look good on any screen (tablet, phone, computer). There are many ways to do this, but three in particular are quite common:

  1. Flexbox
  2. Bootstrap
  3. The Grid

This week we'll go over Flexbox. If we have time this semester we'll learn how to work with the Bootstrap API. Those are by far the most popular. Creating a decent layout is perhaps the most confusing/frustrating parts of web development -- especially at the beginning. So it's important to spend adequate time building a strong foundation before jumping into more complex projects. I really recommend going through this tutorial to learn the basics of layout using floats and setting margins. We are not covering this together, but the tutorial is gold.

Flexbox
  • Pro's
    1. It's supported by most browsers
    2. It's dynamic, meaning that each flex items (ie the children of the flex container) will shrink or expand according to the size of your screen.
    3. Once you get the hang of it, it's easy to use.
  • Con's
    1. It may be difficult to learn at first, but this isn't really a Con because we're going to go through everything you need to know to get started together!
